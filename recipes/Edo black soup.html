Edo black soup

Description

Black soup originally called OMOEBE is from the people of Edo state.it is not a fancy kind of soup but very nutritious, medicinal and tasty.it shares alot of similaritieswith Edikakong from the people of Calabar. Some persons make theirs with just one leaf (bitterleaf). If you want to take this soup to another level then endeavour to add any of the listed leafs aside from pumpkin leafs.

Ingriedents
  • Bunch Scent leaf
  • Bunch Pumpkin leafs
  • Bunch Uziza(hot leaf)
  • Bunch Ebeewuwa
  • Bunch bitterleaf (parboiled before use)
  • Palmnut extract(optional)
  • seasoning
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 2/3 Cooking Spoon palm oil
  • okporoko(also called maureen's fish)
  • crayfish and smoked fish
  • iru
  • Goat head pieced or other meats of choice

steps
  1. Choose three from the listed leafs Wash and blend the leafs with the crayfish and pepper to a smooth paste using water.
  2. If using bitterleaf boil with potash and rinse it before blending
  3. Cook the meats with water and add the blended leaves to the meat stock
  4. Add palm oil, salt, pieced bony fish, seasoning to taste
  5. The consistency for the soup shouldn't be a bit light and not thick.
  6. For that real taste of this soup choose from scent leaf, Uziza, bitterleaf, ebeewuwa.
